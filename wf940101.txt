ECONOMY 
Overview: Guinea-Bissau ranks among the poorest countries in the world, 
with a per capita GDP of roughly $200. Agriculture and fishing are the main 
economic activities. Cashew nuts, peanuts, and palm kernels are the primary 
exports.  Exploitation of known mineral deposits is unlikely at present because 
of a weak infrastructure and the high cost of development. The government's 
four-year plan (1988-91) targeted agricultural development as the top 
priority.
