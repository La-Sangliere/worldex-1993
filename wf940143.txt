ECONOMY 
Overview: The economy is based largely on tourism (including gambling) and 
textile and fireworks manufacturing. Efforts to diversify have spawned other 
small industries - toys, artificial flowers, and electronics. The tourist 
sector has accounted for roughly 25% of GDP, and the clothing industry has 
provided about two-thirds of export earnings; the gambling industry 
represented well over 40% of GDP in 1992. Macau depends on China for most of 
its food, fresh water, and energy imports. Japan and Hong Kong are the main 
suppliers of raw materials and capital goods.
