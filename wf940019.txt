ECONOMY 
Overview: The Bahamas is a stable, middle-income, developing nation whose 
economy is based primarily on tourism and offshore banking. Tourism alone 
provides about 50% of GDP and directly or indirectly employs about 50,000 
people or 40% of the local work force. The economy has slackened in recent 
years, as the annual increase in the number of tourists slowed. Nonetheless, 
per capita GDP is one of the highest in the region.
