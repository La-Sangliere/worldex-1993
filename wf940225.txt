ECONOMY 
Overview: Tokelau's small size, isolation, and lack of resources greatly 
restrain economic development and confine agriculture to the subsistence 
level. The people must rely on aid from New Zealand to maintain public 
services, annual aid being substantially greater than GDP. The principal 
sources of revenue come from sales of copra, postage stamps, souvenir coins, 
and handicrafts. Money is also remitted to families from relatives in New 
Zealand.
