ECONOMY 
Overview: The economy is based on family and corporate agriculture, which 
accounts for 26% of GDP, employs about 60% of the labor force, and supplies 
two-thirds of exports. Manufacturing, predominantly in private hands, accounts 
for about 18% of GDP and 12% of the labor force. In both 1990 and 1991, the 
economy grew by 3%, the fourth and fifth consecutive years of mild growth. In 
1992 growth picked up to 4% as government policies favoring competition and 
foreign trade and investment took stronger hold.
