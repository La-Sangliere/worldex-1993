ECONOMY 
Overview: The economy is based on fishing, tourism, and shipping. 
Agriculture is limited to the production of a few subsistence crops that 
provide only 10% of food requirements. Fishing is the largest industry, 
employing 25% of the work force and accounting for over 60% of exports; it is 
also an important source of government revenue. During the 1980s tourism 
became one of the most important and highest growth sectors of the economy. In 
1988 industry accounted for about 5% of GDP. Real GDP is officially estimated 
to have increased by about 10% annually during the period 1974-90.
