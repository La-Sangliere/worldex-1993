ECONOMY 
Overview: With its long history of semifeudalistic social controls, 
dependence on volatile prices for its mineral exports, and bouts of 
hyperinflation, Bolivia has remained one of the poorest and least developed 
Latin American countries. Since August 1989, President PAZ Zamora, despite his 
Marxist origins, has maintained a moderate policy of repressing domestic 
terrorism, containing inflation, and achieving annual GDP growth of 3 to 4%. 
For many farmers, who constitute half of the country's work force, the main 
cash crop is coca, which is sold for cocaine processing.
