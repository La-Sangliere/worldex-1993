ECONOMY 
Overview: The Malaysian economy, a mixture of private enterprise and a 
soundly managed public sector, has posted a remarkable record of 8%-9% average 
growth in 1987-92. This growth has resulted in a substantial reduction in 
poverty and a marked rise in real wages. Despite sluggish growth in the major 
world economies in 1992, demand for Malaysian goods remained strong and 
foreign investors continued to commit large sums in the economy. The 
government is aware of the inflationary potential of this rapid development 
and is closely monitoring fiscal and monetary policies.
