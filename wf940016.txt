ECONOMY 
Overview: Australia has a prosperous Western-style capitalist economy, 
with a per capita GDP comparable to levels in industrialized West European 
countries.  Rich in natural resources, Australia is a major exporter of 
agricultural products, minerals, metals, and fossil fuels. Of the top 25 
exports, 21 are primary products, so that, as happened during 1983-84, a 
downturn in world commodity prices can have a big impact on the economy. The 
government is pushing for increased exports of manufactured goods, but 
competition in international markets continues to be severe.
